# Chemistry Answer Grading Task

Evaluate the student's chemistry answer against the provided answer key using domain-specific expertise. Your grading must be rigorous, fair, and pedagogically sound.

## Grading Approach

**Conceptual Understanding (40%)**
Assess whether the student grasps underlying chemical principles. Look for correct reasoning about molecular behavior, reaction mechanisms, periodic trends, and thermodynamic principles. Partial credit is awarded for correct conceptual framework even if execution has minor errors.

**Quantitative Accuracy (30%)**
Verify all calculations: stoichiometry, unit conversions, equilibrium constants, pH calculations, thermochemistry. Check significant figures, proper use of scientific notation, and dimensional analysis. A correct setup with arithmetic error receives more credit than a wrong approach with lucky numbers.

**Chemical Notation and Communication (20%)**
Evaluate proper use of chemical formulas, equation balancing, nomenclature (IUPAC), Lewis structures, and electron configurations. Assess clarity of written explanations and logical flow of problem-solving steps.

**Application and Reasoning (10%)**
Determine if the student can apply concepts to novel situations, predict outcomes, and justify answers with chemical logic rather than memorization.

## Chemistry-Specific Checks

**Stoichiometry Verification**
- Balanced equation with correct coefficients and states of matter
- Proper mole ratio application from balanced equation
- Limiting reagent identification when applicable
- Theoretical yield and percent yield calculations
- Unit conversions using dimensional analysis

**Chemical Equations and Reactions**
- Correct products for reaction type (synthesis, decomposition, single/double replacement, combustion, redox)
- Proper oxidation state assignments in redox reactions
- Spectator ions identified in net ionic equations
- Reaction conditions noted when relevant (heat, catalyst, solvent)

**Molecular Structure and Bonding**
- Valid Lewis structures with correct electron distribution
- Formal charges calculated and minimized appropriately
- VSEPR geometry predictions matching electron domain count
- Hybridization states consistent with molecular geometry
- Polarity assessments based on geometry and electronegativity

**Thermodynamics and Equilibrium**
- Sign conventions for ΔH, ΔS, ΔG correctly applied
- Equilibrium expressions with proper exponents from balanced equation
- Le Chatelier predictions consistent with stress applied
- Spontaneity assessments using ΔG or comparing Q to K

**Acid-Base Chemistry**
- pH/pOH calculations with correct logarithmic relationships
- Ka/Kb values used appropriately for weak acids/bases
- Buffer calculations using Henderson-Hasselbalch when applicable
- Conjugate acid-base pairs correctly identified

**Significant Figures and Units**
- Sig figs match least precise measurement in calculation
- Final answer rounded appropriately (not intermediate steps)
- Units carried through calculations and cancel correctly
- Scientific notation used for very large/small numbers

## Common Error Patterns to Identify

- Confusing moles with molecules or grams
- Balancing equations by changing subscripts instead of coefficients
- Forgetting to square/cube concentrations in equilibrium expressions
- Misapplying sig fig rules (especially with logarithms and exact numbers)
- Reversing endothermic/exothermic or spontaneous/nonspontaneous
- Drawing Lewis structures that violate octet rule without justification
- Using wrong molar mass or Avogadro's number
- Mixing up oxidation and reduction (LEO says GER)

## Feedback Strategy

Identify the root cause of errors: Is it a conceptual misunderstanding, a calculation mistake, a notation error, or incomplete reasoning? Provide targeted guidance that helps the student self-correct while building chemical intuition.
